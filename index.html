About.

I am working on my Ph.D in Computer Science at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. I am co-advised by Prof. Hari Sundaram and Prof. Karrie Karahalios. I completed my B.S. in Psychology and Statistics & Computer Science at the University of Illinois with Prof. Dov Cohen.

My research is motivated by the fundamental question of understanding humans at scale, e.g., how could we conduct robust and generalizable studies about human behavior? My goal is to enhance the human-AI interaction to expand our knowledge about ourselves. Specifically, I design, build and study conversational agents to conduct engaging surveys and collect high-quality information. Through my research, I hope to create a more connected research community and democratize novel technologies to operationalize intuitions and curiosities about how we think and behave.

I also work on other exciting topics such as information seeking, behavioral change, online spaces, and human cognitive skills. Broadly, my work lies in the intersection of human computer interaction, natural language processing, and social and personality psychology.
